Banking & Career Insights

Everyday Banking vs Big Finance

An educational finance presentation explaining the structural, operational, and career differences between retail banking functions and high finance domains such as investment banking, valuation, M&A advisory, and capital markets.

Article Overview

This presentation was created to simplify one of the most misunderstood areas in finance careers — the difference between traditional banking operations and high finance roles.

The article breaks down how banks differ in terms of business models, clients, revenue generation, skill requirements, and long-term career trajectories.

Key Insights Covered

  • Difference between retail banking and investment banking
  • Revenue models across lending, advisory, and capital markets
  • Skill requirements including valuation, modelling, and analysis
  • Career progression paths within high finance roles
  • Role of financial modelling in investment banking and advisory

Why This Matters

Many finance aspirants often confuse all banking roles under a single category, despite major differences in responsibilities, analytical depth, compensation structures, and career progression.

This article was designed to create practical clarity for students and professionals exploring careers across banking, valuation, corporate finance, and investment analysis.
