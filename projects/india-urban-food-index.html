India Urban Food Index — Eaglewood Capital

Worked as a Buy-Side Research Analyst at Eaglewood Capital, a Mauritius-based investment platform, contributing to fortnightly macro-consumer research reports analysing urban food inflation, supply-chain disruptions, policy developments, and CPI-linked consumption trends across India.

Research Overview

The India Urban Food Index was designed as a real-time inflation and consumption tracking framework monitoring price movements across a standardised urban food basket covering 21 major Indian cities.

The objective was to identify emerging inflationary pressures, supply-side disruptions, policy transmission effects, and macroeconomic risks before their reflection within official CPI releases.

Research outputs were prepared on a fortnightly basis and focused on generating actionable macro insights for globally oriented investment analysis.

Role & Responsibilities

  • Worked as a Buy-Side Research Analyst supporting macro-consumer and inflation-focused research initiatives.
  • Tracked food inflation, commodity pricing trends, logistics disruptions, and agricultural supply-side developments across India.
  • Analysed macroeconomic developments involving RBI policy commentary, monsoon forecasts, fertiliser pricing, and energy-market disruptions.
  • Prepared institutional-style research commentary integrating quantitative price analysis with policy interpretation and geopolitical developments.
  • Documented supporting references and source validation using Bloomberg, Reuters, RBI publications, Economic Times, CNBC, PIB releases, IMD data, and government agricultural updates.

Macro & Inflation Analysis

The April 23 research update identified a sharp 6.1% week-on-week rise in the India Urban Food Index (₹883 → ₹937), reversing the prior easing trend and signalling renewed inflationary pressure within urban consumption categories. :contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0}

The surge was not broad-based inflation but was concentrated across three major categories — Milk, Chicken, and Tomato — driven by distinct supply-side and logistics disruptions involving heatwaves, feed-cost inflation, and West Asia shipping disruptions.

Volatility increased from 47.62 to 54.39, moving above the long-term average of 48.77 and indicating increasing price instability across tracked categories. :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}

Key Findings & Insights

  • Tomato prices increased nearly 55% YoY across northern cities due to heatwave-driven supply shortages and reduced arrival quality.
  • Chicken prices increased materially as feed-cost inflation and Strait of Hormuz logistics disruptions elevated retail procurement costs.
  • Milk prices experienced a sharp upward repricing following seasonal fodder shortages and rising cold-chain transportation expenses.
  • Fertiliser prices surged significantly, with India receiving urea import offers near $1,000 per metric tonne amid West Asia disruptions.
  • RBI policy interpretation suggested reduced near-term probability of interest-rate cuts due to inflation persistence and monsoon uncertainty.
  • Stable pulses and onion pricing indicated that government intervention and buffer-stock management remained effective for staple categories.

Research Methodology

  • Tracked real-time retail pricing data across 21 Indian cities
  • Analysed CPI-linked inflation transmission dynamics
  • Monitored agricultural supply-chain indicators and reservoir data
  • Integrated monsoon forecasts, El Niño developments, and fertiliser pricing trends
  • Evaluated RBI policy commentary and macroeconomic implications
  • Used Excel-based quantitative analysis and structured macro trend tracking
  • Validated findings through government releases and institutional financial media references

Research analysis incorporated verified macroeconomic and industry references including Bloomberg, Reuters, RBI commentary, CNBC, Economic Times, PIB releases, IMD monsoon forecasts, reservoir data, agricultural production updates, and commodity pricing developments.

Supporting research documentation and references were maintained systematically within each report to ensure institutional-grade research traceability and policy validation standards.
