Project Orion — M&A Advisory Case Study

Developed a sell-side M&A advisory case study involving confidential teaser preparation, strategic buyer screening, due diligence framework development, and acquisition rationale analysis for a digital transformation and process automation company.

Project Overview

The project simulated a live investment banking sell-side process for “Project Orion,” a mid-sized India-based digital transformation and business process automation platform serving global enterprise clients.

The assignment focused on preparing institutional-style M&A deliverables including a confidential teaser, buyer universe analysis, strategic fit assessment, diligence framework, and outreach communication strategy.

Business & Industry Analysis

Project Orion operated an asset-light digital transformation model focused on automation, data integration, workflow optimisation, and custom application development across manufacturing, healthcare, distribution, and consumer sectors.

The company positioned itself as a mid-market specialist offering customised and cost-efficient enterprise automation solutions compared to larger standardised IT service providers.

Analysis highlighted the scalability advantages of recurring client relationships, operational flexibility, and increasing enterprise demand for automation-led efficiency solutions.

Financial & Operational Assessment

  • Estimated annual revenue analysed within a ₹15–25 crore range based on employee scale and industry benchmarks.
  • Business model evaluated as asset-light with scalable delivery economics and recurring client engagement visibility.
  • Operational scalability supported by a 500+ employee base and diversified industry exposure.
  • EBITDA profile assessed positively due to high-margin automation and integration service offerings.
  • Strong long-term growth visibility identified through increasing global enterprise automation adoption trends.

Buyer Screening & Strategic Fit Analysis

Developed a strategic buyer universe based on automation capabilities, integration capacity, India delivery presence, sector exposure, and acquisition appetite within the digital transformation ecosystem.

Potential acquirers included Infosys, Tech Mahindra, Cognizant, LTIMindtree, Siemens, Blackstone, Carlyle, and Bain Capital across both strategic and private equity buyer categories.

Top buyers were shortlisted based on strategic alignment, operational synergies, automation capabilities, and integration scalability.

Due Diligence Framework

Built a structured data-readiness and diligence framework covering financials, client concentration, operational scalability, legal compliance, technology infrastructure, management dependency, and growth projections.

The framework replicated practical lower-middle-market M&A diligence preparation processes commonly used during sell-side transaction execution.

Key Findings & Strategic Insights

  • Automation and workflow optimisation demand continue benefiting mid-market enterprise technology providers.
  • Asset-light IT service models create strong scalability and margin advantages within digital transformation businesses.
  • Mid-market specialists remain attractive acquisition targets for larger IT firms seeking niche capability expansion.
  • Healthcare, manufacturing, and consumer sectors represent strong long-term automation adoption verticals.
  • Private equity interest remains high for scalable recurring-revenue IT service businesses with operational leverage potential.

Role & Responsibilities

  • Conducted strategic buyer screening analysis

  • Drafted institutional-style outreach communication
  • Structured M&A advisory style presentation deliverables
